Emma also hosts a weekly radio show called "Imaginary Landscapes" through her school radio station, WNYU 89.1. The show airs each Sunday from 12-2pm on wnyu.org.

Imaginary Landscapes focuses more on how pieces are composed than on the end product. Listen to hear experimentally composed music released in the past 10 years, with specific attention on female artists and artists of color. Types of composition styles include graphic scores, glitch composition, stochastic music, site specific composition, and less prevalent styles that are unique to only one composer. The title comes from John Cage's piece for multiple radios also entitled Imaginary Landscapes. So.. radio, and experimental composition. Haha.
